Frequently Asked Questions

How do I place an order?

Just place an order. Simple as that.

What is Repair?

Our Repair service focuses on removing unwanted signals present in your audio. This includes the reduction of extraneous noise such as ventilation or wind. Our engineers can also reduce signal bleed from other instruments. For example, if there is too much hi-hat signal in your snare recording, we will reduce the amount of hi-hat that is audible in your snare recording without affecting the tonal quality of the snare.

What is Performance Correction?

Performance Correction includes the adjustment of pitch in case a note deviates from the tonal center established by the arrangement. Special care is taken to ensure that notes which are off-key on purpose are retained, while notes that are accidentaly performed too flat or sharp are corrected.
Note that this is not auto-tune, as each note is tuned manually according to the composition.

If you request Performance Correction along with another service (mixing, mastering, repair), you will receive a version of your results with Performance Correction and a version without. This is in addition to the two versions that are included with mixing and mastering. Therefore, you will receive a total of four results when you select this option.

What if I want a revision?

Your order includes one revision coupon. If you would like to make changes to your results, navigate to www.vectorflux.com/order/revision and enter your order ID, coupon code, and your prefered version of the results (A or B). Along with this information, describe the changes you would like to have made (e.g. "I would like the bass to be less prominent."). Our engineers will address your comments and you will receive a revised result.

What type of audio files can I upload?

Only WAV files are currently accepted. If you have a different file format such as FLAC or OGG, please convert this into one of the aforementioned formats before placing your order.

What is the Reference field for?

The reference field is for you to list songs or artists that you would like to model your songs after. Our engineers will listen to the music you list here and mix and master your project in a similar style while retaining a unique sound based on your composition.

How much will my project cost to mix?

Prices depend on the number of tracks, the length of the project, and whether you request mastering.
